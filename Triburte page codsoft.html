Priyanka Chopra Jonas

Indian actress and Producer

About her Early life and Education

Priyanka Chopra was born on 18 July 1982 in Jamshedpur, Bihar (present-day Jharkhand), to Ashok and Madhu Chopra, both physicians in the Indian Army. Her father was a Punjabi Hindu from Ambala. Her mother, Madhu Chopra was a Bihari-Magahi Hindu from Jharkhand.Chopra has a brother, Siddharth, who is seven years her junior. Bollywood actresses Parineeti Chopra, Meera Chopra, and Mannara Chopra are cousins. Owing to Chopra's parents' professions as military physicians, the family was posted in a number of places in India, including Delhi, Chandigarh, Ambala, Ladakh, Lucknow, Bareilly, and Pune. Among the schools she attended were La Martiniere Girls' School in Lucknow and St. Maria Goretti College in Bareilly.She later moved to the United States for a few years, where she attended schools in Massachusetts, Iowa, and New York. Chopra returned to India and completed her high school education at the Army Public School in Bareilly.

Career Beginnigs of Priyanka Chopra

Priyanka Chopra Jonas's career began with her foray into the world of beauty pageants and modeling, which subsequently led her to the film industry.

Miss India and Miss World 2000: Priyanka Chopra's journey to fame began when she participated in the Femina Miss India contest in 2000. She finished as the first runner-up, which qualified her to represent India at the Miss World pageant. At the age of 18, she won the Miss World title in 2000, bringing her international recognition and opening doors to various opportunities in the entertainment industry.ntry into Bollywood

Film Debut: Following her Miss World victory, Priyanka received several offers from the Indian film industry. She made her Bollywood debut in 2003 with "The Hero: Love Story of a Spy", alongside Sunny Deol and Preity Zinta. Though the film had moderate success, it was a significant stepping stone for her career.

Early Roles: In the same year, she starred in "Andaaz" opposite Akshay Kumar and Lara Dutta, which was a commercial success and earned her the Filmfare Award for Best Female Debut. Her performance was well-received, and she began to establish herself as a talented newcomer in the industry.

Critical Acclaim: Priyanka's role in "Aitraaz" (2004) marked a turning point in her career. Playing a negative character, she received critical acclaim and won the Filmfare Award for Best Performance in a Negative Role. This performance showcased her versatility and ability to handle complex characters.

Commercial Success: Following "Aitraaz", she starred in several successful films such as "Mujhse Shaadi Karogi" (2004), "Waqt: The Race Against Time" (2005), and "Krrish" (2006). These films not only did well at the box office but also helped cement her status as a leading actress in Bollywood.

Diverse Roles: Over the next few years, Priyanka continued to take on diverse roles in films like "Don" (2006), where she played a tough cop opposite Shah Rukh Khan, and "Fashion" (2008), a film that earned her the National Film Award for Best Actress. Her portrayal of an ambitious model facing the ups and downs of the fashion industry was widely praised.

Experimentation: Priyanka didn't shy away from experimenting with different genres and characters. She appeared in romantic comedies, dramas, and action films, continually expanding her acting range and appeal.

Film Roles: She has appeared in several Hollywood films, including "Baywatch" (2017), "A Kid Like Jake" (2018), and "Isn't It Romantic" (2019).

Singing: In addition to acting, Priyanka has ventured into music, releasing singles like "In My City" featuring will.i.am and "Exotic" featuring Pitbull.

Achievemts, Awards and Recognition

Priyanka Chopra Jonas has received numerous awards and recognitions throughout her career for her contributions to film, television, and her humanitarian efforts. Chopra has won a National Film Award for Best Actress for Fashion (2008) and five Filmfare Awards: Best Female Debut for Andaaz (2003), Best Performance in a Negative Role for Aitraaz (2004), Best Actress for Fashion (2008), Critics Award for Best Actress for 7 Khoon Maaf (2011), and Best Supporting Actress for Bajirao Mastani (2015). She has also won two People's Choice Awards: "Favourite Actress In A New TV Series", and "Favorite Dramatic TV Actress" for Quantico.She is the first South Asian actress to win a People's Choice Award.In 2016, she was awarded the Padma Shri, the fourth highest civilian award, by the Government of India for her contribution to arts and was honored as one of the BBC 100 Women in 2022.In 2021, Priyanka released her memoir, "Unfinished", which received positive reviews and provided insights into her personal and professional life. She runs her own production company, Purple Pebble Pictures, which focuses on promoting regional Indian cinema.
